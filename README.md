## Project Overview

City Under Pressure is a strategic governance simulator that models the complexity of sustainable urban decision-making under limited resources.

Players take on the role of a city leader managing:

- Financial constraints  
- Urban crises  
- Public trust  
- Policy implementation  

The core objective of the game is to **maximize Reputation** while maintaining financial stability and surviving limited administrative capacity.

Reputation represents institutional trust and public confidence.  
Every decision affects how citizens perceive the legitimacy and effectiveness of governance.

---

## How to Play

You begin with limited funds and 50 actions.

The game contains four governance decks representing different aspects of city management. Each turn, you interact with one card at a time.

There are three types of cards:

- **Events** – Urban crises that must be resolved.
- **Policies** – Strategic governance decisions with financial trade-offs.
- **Mini-Games** – Civic participation simulations.

### Controls

- Swipe right to use a card.
- Swipe left to discard a **Policy** or **Mini-Game** card.
- Discarding does **not** consume actions.
- **Events cannot be discarded**; swiping either direction resolves the event.

### Win and Loss Conditions

Your administration must:

- Avoid financial collapse (Funds must not reach 0).
- Manage all four card decks.
- Operate within 50 actions.

The game ends if:

- Funds reach 0  
- Actions reach 0  
- All card decks are exhausted  

Your final score is determined by your **Reputation**.

Higher Reputation reflects stronger institutional legitimacy and public trust.

## Core Gameplay Mechanics

### Events
Urban crises such as infrastructure failures, environmental hazards, and public controversies.

Events:
- Reduce funds  
- Pressure governance stability  
- Require active resolution  

They simulate real-world systemic stress within cities.

---

### Policies
Strategic governance decisions that involve trade-offs between:

- Immediate financial cost  
- Long-term public trust  
- Institutional credibility  

Policies can increase Reputation but consume limited resources.

---

### Civic Participation Simulation (Mini-Games)

Mini-games represent citizen-government interaction at a micro level.

They simulate public engagement, transparency, and ethical decision-making.

#### Waste Sorting Challenge
Models citizen-level environmental awareness.  
Aligned with SDG 11 by reinforcing sustainable urban practices.

#### Public Live Q&A
Simulates governmental transparency under public scrutiny.  
Aligned with SDG 16 through accountability and public communication.

#### Civic Judgment System
Models ethical trade-offs in policy decisions, balancing justice, economic impact, and social stability.

Each mini-game directly influences Reputation and funds.
